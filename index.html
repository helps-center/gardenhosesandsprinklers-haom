​🌿 GARDEN gear: HOSES &amp; SPRINKLERS

**💧 GARDEN HOSES**

1.  Hose Types:

    *   Vinyl: Cheap and light, good for easy watering.
    *   Rubber: Lasts, bends well, great for tough jobs and all weather.
    *   Reinforced: Extra strong to handle more water pressure.
    *   Expandable: Shrinks for easy storage, grows with water.
    *   Soaker: Waters slowly at the roots. Awesome for plants.
    *   Flat: Stores easy, good for lower water flow.
2.  What Hoses Are Made Of:

    *   Vinyl: Cheap but can break or bend.
    *   Rubber: Lasts long, good any weather.
    *   Polyurethane: Light, safe for drinking water, doesn't bend easy.
    *   PVC: Stiff when it's cold but lasts.
3.  How Long &amp; Wide?

    *   Lengths: Usually 25, 50, 75, or 100 ft.
    *   Width: Normally ⅝, ½, or ¾.
        *   ⅝: Common size, good flow and pressure.
        *   ½: Light, easy to use, less water flow.
        *   ¾: More water, good for big gardens.
4.  Hooking It Up:

    *   What it's Made Of: Brass (lasts), plastic (light), or aluminum (light, but can rust).
    *   Types:
        *   Threaded: Normal screw-on type.
        *   Quick-Connect: Pops on easy.
        *   Washers: Rubber rings to stop leaks.
5.  Cool Things to Look For:

    *   Doesn't Kink: More layers or stronger hoses don't bend easy.
    *   Burst Strength: How much pressure before it breaks. Higher is better.
    *   UV proof: So the sun doesn't ruin it.
    *   Safe to Drink From: No lead or bad stuff inside.
6.  Taking Care of Your Hose:

    *   Don't bend it too much.
    *   Empty it after with it to stop freezing or mold.
    *   Store on a reel or hanger.
    *   Keep out of the sun.

**SPRINKLERS**

1.  Kind of Sprinklers:

    *   Oscillating: Sprays in a square. Good for normal lawns.
    *   Rotary/Rotating: Spins around. Great for round areas.
    *   Stationary (Spot): Sprays one spot. Works for small areas.
    *   Impulse (Impact): Shoots water far. Covers big, bumpy yards.
    *   Traveling: Moves itself. Good for big yards.
    *   Misting: Fine spray. good for plants that need gentle water.
    *   In-Ground Pop-Up: Stays in the ground. Common for homes.
2.  What They're Made Of:

    *   Plastic: Cheap, but might break.
    *   Metal: Tougher, good if you use it a lot.
    *   Both: Plastic and metal for good price and life.
3.  How Much Does It Cover?

    *   Spray Far: How far the water goes.
    *   Spray Shape: Round, half-round, square, etc.
    *   Change It: Adjust how far, wide, and where it sprays.
4.  Water Power:

    *   Pressure: Most work around 30–60 PSI.
    *   How Much Water: Know how much you're using. Saves water.
5.  Timers:

    *   Manual: Simple timer you turn.
    *   Electronic: Set times and how long.
    *   Smart: Uses Wi-Fi and weather to water right.
6.  Keep It Working:

    *   Clean any clogs in the holes.
    *   Flush the system to clean it out.
    *   Empty in-ground systems for the winter to stop breaks.

**🔧 Extras:**

*   Hose Reels: Keeps hoses neat.
*   Splitters: Use two hoses from one tap.
*   Backflow Thing: Stops dirty water from getting in your drinking water.
*   Timers &amp; Sensors: Saves water.
*   Stands: Raises sprinklers for bigger plants.
*   Hose Guides: Stops hoses from hurting plants.

**🌱 Save Water:**

*   Water early or late to stop water from drying up.
*   Soaker hoses water the roots good.
*   Put plants that need same water together.
*   Use mulch to keep soil wet.
